Cold brew is coffee steeped in cold water for 12-24 hours. The result is smoother and less acidic than hot-brewed coffee, with a naturally sweeter taste. We tested seven ratios across three months and these are the picks we recommend for home use.

For best results, use a 1:8 ratio of coarse-ground coffee to filtered water and steep at room temperature for sixteen hours before refrigerating. Stronger ratios produce concentrate you can dilute with milk or water; weaker ratios give you a ready-to-drink brew without further preparation.

Equipment

You need a wide-mouth jar, a fine filter, and patience. A burr grinder helps but is optional.

Recipe

Combine grounds and water, stir, cover, and steep. Strain twice through a paper filter for a clean finish.
