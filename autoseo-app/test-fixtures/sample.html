Sourdough starter from scratch

A sourdough starter is a living culture of flour and water that captures wild yeast and bacteria from the environment. Once active, it leavens bread with rich flavor and no commercial yeast required. It takes about a week to build a healthy starter from kitchen ingredients.

You only need two ingredients to begin: unbleached all-purpose flour and filtered water. Whole wheat or rye flour can accelerate fermentation, but plain flour works too. Avoid chlorinated tap water — it suppresses the wild microbes.

Day-by-day schedule

Mix equal weights of flour and water in a jar each day, discarding most of the previous day's contents to keep the culture fresh and food-supply ratios right.
